New Bay and Bow Replacement Windows

Boost your home’s curb appeal with new bay or bow windows. These window options add a stunning aesthetic and natural light to any space. A bay window protrudes from the outer wall of your home and is made of three individual window units that come together at equal angles. A bow window extends from a home’s structure and typically includes four to six panes alongside each other. This design gives a bow window a smooth, curved appearance.


What is the difference between Bay and Bow Windows?

Bow windows are similar to bay windows with the difference being that:
  • Bay Windows has fewer panes, usually a larger picture window in the center, with two smaller windows on either side
  • Bay Windows have a more angular, boxier appearance and fewer panes than bow windows. Bow windows have a more graceful curve associated with their look.
  • Bow windows are an excellent option for adding charm, character, and curb appeal. While they can add natural light and brightness to any room, they also provide additional space that can be used for seating, storage and more
